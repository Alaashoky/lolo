# Trading Strategies

This document outlines various trading strategies for the Lolo project.

## 1. Trend Following
- **Description**: This strategy involves buying securities that are in an upward trend and selling those that are in a downward trend.
- **Implementation**: Use moving averages to identify trends.

## 2. Mean Reversion
- **Description**: This strategy is based on the belief that prices will revert to their historical average over time.
- **Implementation**: Identify overbought or oversold conditions using indicators like RSI.

## 3. Momentum Trading
- **Description**: This strategy seeks to capitalize on the continuance of existing trends.
- **Implementation**: Trade based on the speed or momentum of price movements.

## 4. Arbitrage
- **Description**: This strategy involves taking advantage of price differences between markets.
- **Implementation**: Buy low in one market and sell high in another.

## 5. Breakout Strategies
- **Description**: This strategy involves entering a position once the price breaks above a resistance level or below a support level.
- **Implementation**: Set alerts on significant price levels to catch breakouts.

## 6. Scalping
- **Description**: This strategy focuses on making small profits on a large number of trades.
- **Implementation**: Execute quick trades based on small price changes.

## Conclusion
- Each strategy has its own risk and reward profile. Traders should ensure to backtest any strategy before implementation in live markets.
